Stroke or Not? That is the question

Stroke, also called cerebrovascular accident(CVA) occurs when part of the brain loses its blood supply and the part of the body that the blood-deprived brain cells control stops working. This loss of blood supply can be ischemic because of lack of blood flow, or hemorrhagic because of bleeding into brain tissue.

Our project titled “Towards early Stroke prediction using effective stroke predictors” focuses on early diagnosis of stroke by monitoring people’s periodic physiological data and designing useful stroke predictors when the symptoms are not apparent.

Our project is categorized into two phases :

  • 1. Understanding the contributors - Identifying the most effective predictors from the plausible factors.
  • 2. Accurately predict if a person might have a stroke.

Timely prediction of the occurrence of stroke will be a significant contribution towards preventing long-term patient disability and improving public health. The target demographic is individuals of age 40 and above.

'Climb125' = Does your health now limit you in this activity, and if so, how much: Climbing one flight of stairs
(Limited a lot, limited a little, not limited at all)

'Bathe25' = Quality of Life (QOL) (Sleep Heart Health Study Visit One (SHHS1)): Health limits bathing and dressing
(Limited a lot, limited a little, not limited at all)

'Rest10' = Quality of sleep: restless/restful (1=restless to 5=restful)

'Modact25' = Does your health now limit you in this activity, and if so, how much: Moderate activities, such as moving a table, pushing a vacuum cleaner, bowling, or playing golf
(Limited a lot, limited a little, not limited at all)

'Lift25' = Health limits Lifting or carrying groceries
(Limited a lot, limited a little, not limited at all)

'Climbs25' = Health limits climbing several flights of stairs
(Limited a lot, limited a little, not limited at all)

'Hlthlm25' = Health limited your social activities
(All of the time, Most of the time, Some of the time, A little of the time, None of the time)

Observed

'Systbp' = Seated Systolic Blood Pressure (BP)

'Diasbp' = Seated Diastolic Blood Pressure

'Minfa10' = How many minutes did it take for you to fall asleep at bedtime last night? (number of minutes, up to 3 digits)

'Cgrtts10' = How many cigarettes 4 hours before going to sleep last night? (number, up to 2 digits)

'Shots10' = How many mixed drinks or shots of liquor 4 hours before going to sleep last night?

'Bmi_s1' = body mass index. bmi_s1 = ( weight / ( (height/100) * (height/100) ) )

'Weight' = weight in kilograms

'Height' = height in centimeters

'Age_s1' = Age at time of test

Calculated

'rawpf_s1' = Physical Functioning Raw Score

'rawgh_s1' = General Health Perceptions Raw Score

'rawvt_s1' = Vitality Raw Score

'rawbp_s1' = Pain Index Raw Score

'mh_s1' = Mental Health Index Standardized Score

'gh_s1' = General Health Perceptions Standardized Score

'pcs_s1' = Physical Component Scale Standardized Score

'mcs_s1' = Mental Component Scale Standardized Score

ECG

'Ventrate' = Ventricular rate
